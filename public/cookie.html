Cookie Policy

Effective Date: November 16, 2025

This Cookie Policy explains how Influzer.ai uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website and services.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and provide information to website owners.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the website to function properly (e.g., session management, authentication)
  • Functional Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings (e.g., language selection)
  • Analytics Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website
  • Marketing Cookies: Used to deliver relevant advertisements and track campaign effectiveness

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Name Purpose Duration
connect.sid Session management and authentication Session / 7 days
language Stores your language preference 1 year
_ga, _gid Google Analytics (if enabled) 2 years / 24 hours

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that set their own cookies:

  • Google Analytics: Helps us analyze website traffic and user behavior
  • Google AdSense: Displays relevant advertisements (for free tier users)
  • OAuth Providers: Google OAuth may set cookies for authentication

These third parties have their own privacy policies and cookie practices. We encourage you to review them.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse or accept cookies. You can also delete cookies that have already been set.
  • Browser Extensions: Various browser extensions can help you manage cookies
  • Opt-Out Tools: You can opt out of certain third-party cookies through their respective opt-out mechanisms

Note: Disabling certain cookies may impact the functionality of our Services.

6. Do Not Track Signals

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites you visit that you do not want to have your online activity tracked. Currently, there is no standard for how DNT signals should be interpreted. We do not currently respond to DNT signals.

7. Updates to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any material changes by posting the new Cookie Policy on this page.

8. Contact Us

If you have questions about our use of cookies, please contact us through our website contact form.
